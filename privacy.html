Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026

ColdPass is designed so that there is nothing to collect. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or store any personal data.

No accounts, no servers

ColdPass has no user accounts and no backend servers. You do not register, sign in, or provide any personal information to use the app.

Your data stays on your device

Everything in your vault — your service entries, the inputs used to derive passwords, your 2FA secrets, and your settings — is created and kept on your device. Passwords themselves are not stored at all; they are derived on demand and held only momentarily in memory while in use.

No analytics, no tracking

The app contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, or third-party tracking SDKs. ColdPass makes no network requests to operate your vault.

When data leaves your device

Data leaves your device only when you explicitly choose to do so — for example, when you export an encrypted backup file or create and share an inheritance/recovery package. In those cases you control the file and how it is delivered. ColdPass is never involved in the transfer and keeps no copy.

Device permissions

  • Camera — used only to scan 2FA (TOTP) QR codes you choose to add. Images are processed on-device and never uploaded.
  • Face ID / Touch ID — used to unlock your vault and authorize sensitive actions. Biometric data is handled entirely by iOS and never seen by ColdPass.
  • Local network — used only when you send a password to the ColdPass browser extension on a computer on your own network. The transfer is end-to-end encrypted and stays on your local network.

Children's privacy

ColdPass is not directed at children and collects no data from anyone.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date.

Your data, your control

Because ColdPass holds no data about you, there is nothing for us to access, export, correct, sell, or hand over. Everything stays on your device, under your control.
